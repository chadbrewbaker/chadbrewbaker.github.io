Consistency checker

Paste the employee handbook, a requirements doc, meeting notes, a contract clause — or a chat log. The on-device language model (Chrome’s built-in Prompt API / Gemini Nano) translates the prose into propositional Horn clauses — the part language models are good at — and an exact forward-chaining engine in 9 KB of WebAssembly decides whether the statements can all be true at once — the part they are not. Verdicts come with a machine-checked derivation tracing each contradiction back to the document’s own quoted words. Nothing leaves your machine, which your legal team will consider the best feature on this page.

How it works, and where it comes from

1 The oldest plan in AI, with new parts

Translate ordinary statements into logic, then let a mechanical procedure draw the consequences: that is McCarthy’s 1959 advice taker proposal [1], and it is exactly this page, with the translation finally done by a language model small enough to live in the browser and the statements finally supplied by the modern corporation, an inexhaustible source. The division of labor follows the neurosymbolic taxonomy Kautz sketched in his AAAI address [2]: the network handles perception of messy language; the symbolic engine handles entailment, where its answer is checkable and the network’s is not. The seam between them — the extracted clause set — is deliberately exposed as an editable text box with verbatim quotes, because that seam is where the errors live.

2 Why Horn clauses, and how inconsistency is even possible

Definite Horn clauses (Horn 1951 [3]; as a programming language, Kowalski 1974 [4]) always have a least model, so by themselves they can never be inconsistent. Contradiction enters through integrity constraints in the deductive-database sense [5]: goal clauses with the distinguished head inconsistent. Negated statements use the dual-rail convention — p and not_p as separate atoms with an automatic constraint inconsistent :- p, not_p. — the same encoding the CVP→Horn P-completeness reduction uses. The engine then runs Dowling–Gallier-style forward chaining [6] to the fixpoint; the document is inconsistent exactly when inconsistent is derived. Every derived atom records the rule and premises that produced it, so the verdict ships with its proof, walked back to asserted facts and their quotes.

3 The engine, and a note on inherent seriality

The solver is the Datalog engine from this site’s Horn clause solver with provenance added: semi-naive evaluation in freestanding C compiled to wasm32, integer-interned atoms, hash-deduplicated tuples, and per-tuple derivation records. Horn satisfiability is decidable in linear time but is P-complete (Jones–Laaser 1977 [7]) — inherently sequential as far as forty years of complexity theory can tell — which is precisely why it is the piece delegated to an exact engine rather than asked of a constant-depth neural forward pass.

4 The Prompt API

Extraction uses the built-in LanguageModel interface [8]: availability check, session with a system prompt, and structured output via a JSON-schema responseConstraint where supported, with a plain-JSON fallback. Chrome 148+ ships it by default on capable desktop hardware; Edge’s Phi-based implementation is currently behind a flag in Canary. The model runs locally and the page makes no network requests after load, so documents stay on the device. When no model is present, steps 2 and 3 work unaided — the checker is honest about which of its two halves is doing the thinking.
